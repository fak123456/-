## Task
Generate **one** image that makes the **material / surface texture** of the **same product** the visual hero, while keeping **the product clearly recognizable**. This is a close-up of the **real product**, not an isolated swatch.

## Framing (important)
- Show enough of the product silhouette to recognize it at a glance (a clear partial view of one identifiable component).
- Product fills roughly **50–70%** of frame; shoot close but natural, then bring the material area forward — do not crop into a flat abstract patch.

## Differentiation (when {type_total} > 1)
- Image 1: the **dominant** primary material (largest visible surface — body, fabric, mesh, frame).
- Image 2: a **secondary** material / accent (gasket, hinge, stitched seam, rubber foot, plastic clip, metal joint, trim).
- The two must show **different parts** (different crop, surface, texture).

## Consistency & quality
- Match reference white balance / color temperature; product color must not drift.
- Raking / slightly directional light to reveal micro-structure (weave, grain, knurl, matte/gloss), exposure stays natural.
- Material close-ups expose defects most — surface must look **just-cleaned and factory-new**: the pattern must be **perfectly regular and uniform** (every cell / strand intact), no debris, no aging.
- Do not invent textures, weaves, perforations, or finishes absent from the references; no text overlays.

## Negative
microscope swatch, abstract texture patch, unrecognizable product, color drift from references, invented surface pattern, broken weave / mesh, missing strands, holes not in design, defects (scratches / dust / lint / fibers / smudges / dents / cracks), used-product look, plasticized CGI, identical crop across the material images, motion blur, text overlay

## Task
Generate **one** close-up of the **same product** as the references, focused on one key functional feature (closure, mechanism, joint, handle, port, control). It must stay a **clearly recognizable product photo for a detail panel**, not a microscope shot.

## Framing (important)
- Keep enough of the product silhouette visible that it's identifiable at a glance — at least the section with the feature plus context.
- Product fills roughly **50–70%** of frame; do not fill the frame with a flat surface.
- Keep the reference's orientation / view; don't invent strange angles.

## Consistency & quality
- Match reference white balance, color temperature, saturation; dominant color must not drift.
- Close-ups expose defects — be extra strict: surface must be clean and even, and any pattern (mesh / weave / knurl) must stay **regular and uniform** (no missing strands, broken cells, or holes not in the design).
- Do not invent details, holes, screws, seams, or features absent from the references; don't change the surface finish.
- Background: clean, slightly out-of-focus neutral or soft white; sharp focus on the feature, gentle natural falloff (no heavy artificial blur).
- No text overlays, annotation arrows, or callouts.

## Negative
microscope-level macro, abstract texture-only crop, unrecognizable product, color drift from main image, invented features, wrong material finish, broken weave, missing strands, holes not in design, defects (scratches / dust / fibers / smudges / dents / cracks), used-product look, cartoon style, plasticized CGI, motion blur, text overlay, busy background

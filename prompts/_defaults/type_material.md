## Task
Generate **one** image that emphasizes the material / surface texture of the **same product** as in the references, while still keeping **the product itself clearly recognizable**.

This is not a microscope shot of a fabric or metal swatch in isolation. It is a close-up of the **real product** in which the chosen surface or material area is the visual hero.

## Framing rule (very important)
- The image must show enough of the product silhouette for a viewer to recognize what the product is at a glance — at least a clear partial view of one identifiable component.
- The product should occupy roughly **50–70%** of the frame.
- Approach: shoot the product from a close but natural distance, then bring the material area forward as the main focus. Do **not** crop into a flat abstract patch.

## Differentiation rule (when {type_total} > 1)
- Image 1 of {type_total}: highlight the **dominant** primary material of the product (the largest visible surface — body, fabric, mesh, frame, etc.).
- Image 2 of {type_total}: highlight a **secondary** material or accent (gasket, hinge, stitched seam, rubber foot, plastic clip, metal joint, mesh edge, fabric trim).
- The two material images must show **different parts** of the product. Different crop, different surface, different texture.

## Color / lighting consistency rule
- Match reference white balance and color temperature exactly. The product color must not drift.
- Use raking or slightly directional light to bring out surface micro-structure (weave, grain, knurl, matte/gloss interplay) — but keep overall exposure natural.

## Hard constraints — keep product identity locked
- Same product as references: same color, materials, geometry, number of parts.
- Do not invent textures, weaves, perforations, or finishes that are not on the real product in the references.
- No fake brand textures, no invented logos, no text overlays.

## Hard constraints — zero defects (highest priority)
- The product must look **factory-new, pristine, flawless**. This is the strictest rule for material close-ups — defects are extremely visible here.
- ABSOLUTELY NO: scratches, dust, lint, fibers, smudges, fingerprints, oil stains, dents, chips, cracks, frayed edges, loose threads, broken weave / mesh / cells, holes that aren't part of the design, uneven paint, color bleeding, wear marks, burrs, faded color, asymmetry, manufacturing flaws of any kind.
- The material pattern (mesh / weave / knurl / grain) must be **perfectly regular and uniform** across the entire visible area. Every cell, every strand, every line must be intact and identically formed.
- The surface must look **just cleaned**, with no debris and no aging.

## Visual style
- Clean, minimal background (the product itself, neutral surface, or soft white).
- Sharp focus on the material area; gentle natural falloff elsewhere.

## Task
Produce the **best possible main listing image** of the **exact same product** as in the reference images, for Amazon EU.

The first reference image is normally the seller's own main image. Treat it as a strong reference for **composition and angle** — if it already presents the product well, **keep the same angle and framing** and only:
- Replace the background with pure white RGB (255,255,255).
- Improve lighting evenness and contact shadow.
- Tighten the crop slightly so the product fills 75–85% of the frame.
- Remove any background distractions, props, watermarks, text labels, or compositing artifacts.

If, and only if, you can clearly choose **a more flattering angle** (e.g., a small rotation up to 15° that better reveals the product's distinguishing features or selling points), you may switch to that angle. Otherwise stay on the reference angle.

## Goal — make this a high-CTR main image
- It must be **instantly readable as the product from the title** — a shopper should know what it is in 1 second.
- It must look **premium, clean, and trustworthy**, like a professional catalog shot. No amateur look.
- It must show the **whole product** clearly: full silhouette, all key features visible. If the product is a set of multiple units, show all units in the same arrangement as the reference.

## Visual
- Background: pure white RGB (255,255,255), perfectly clean.
- Lighting: soft studio lighting from upper-front, very subtle realistic contact shadow under the product.
- Crop: product centered, 75–85% of frame, comfortable margin.
- Style: photorealistic premium catalog. Not illustration, not over-saturated, not plasticized CGI.

## Hard constraints — background must be flawless
- The white background must be a **perfectly clean, even, solid pure white #FFFFFF**, edge to edge.
- ABSOLUTELY NO: background noise, sensor grain, JPEG/compression artifacts, color banding, off-white tints (cream / yellow / grey / blue), gradient backgrounds, spotlight halos, vignettes, dust specks, smudges, fingerprints, scratches, scuffs, hair, fibers, lint, paper texture, fabric texture, brush strokes, or any other visible imperfection on the background.
- The only mark on the background that is allowed is a single very subtle realistic contact shadow directly under the product. No other shadows, no second light sources, no reflections on the background.
- Treat the background as a freshly-rendered solid white canvas, not a photographed seamless paper.

## Hard constraints — identity lock
- Same product shape, geometry, proportions, silhouette as the reference. Do NOT restyle.
- Same color (hex-level), same surface finish (matte/gloss), same materials and textures.
- Same number of units / parts / accessories visible. A set of 4 in the reference must be a set of 4 in the output.
- Same printed labels, codes, or markings exactly as on the reference; do NOT invent new text, logos, badges, QR codes, brand graphics, or stickers.
- No hands, faces, lifestyle props, or environmental context.

## Hard constraints — zero defects (highest priority)
- The product must look **factory-new, pristine, flawless** — like a brand-new sample for an A+ listing photo.
- ABSOLUTELY NO: scratches, dust, smudges, fingerprints, dents, chips, cracks, frayed edges, loose threads, uneven paint, color bleeding, wear marks, burrs, faded color, deformation, asymmetry between identical units, or any other defect.
- If a unit visible in the reference has any imperfection, **clean it up** in the output. Do not propagate dirt or wear.

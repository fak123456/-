## Task
Generate **one** close-up of the **same product** as in the references, focused on one key functional feature (closure, mechanism, joint, handle, port, control surface).

**Critical**: the product must still be **clearly recognizable** in this image. Do NOT zoom in so far that only an abstract texture or surface is visible. Think "close-up product photo for an Amazon detail panel", not "microscope shot".

## Framing rule (very important)
- A significant portion of the product silhouette must remain visible in the frame — at least the section that contains the highlighted feature plus enough surrounding context that the product is identifiable at a glance.
- The product should fill roughly **50–70%** of the frame. Do **not** fill the frame with only a flat surface.
- Keep the same product orientation/style of view as the references (do not invent strange angles).

## Color / lighting consistency rule
This image must look like it was photographed in the same session as the main hero image.
- Match the reference white balance, color temperature, and saturation exactly.
- The dominant product color must remain identical to references — no warming, cooling, fading, or oversaturation.
- Use soft, slightly directional studio light from the upper-left, similar to the references.

## Hard constraints — keep product identity locked
- Same product as references: same color, same materials, same geometry, same number of parts.
- Do **not** invent details, holes, screws, seams, or features that are not visible in the references.
- Do **not** change the surface texture (matte stays matte, woven stays woven, smooth stays smooth).
- **No** added text overlays, no annotation arrows, no callouts.

## Hard constraints — zero defects (highest priority)
- The product must look **factory-new, pristine, flawless**. Close-ups make defects obvious — be especially careful here.
- ABSOLUTELY NO: scratches, dust, fibers, smudges, fingerprints, oil stains, dents, chips, cracks, frayed edges, loose threads, broken weave, uneven paint, color bleeding, wear marks, burrs, faded color, asymmetry, manufacturing flaws.
- Surface must be clean, even, and consistent. Material patterns (mesh, weave, knurl) must be **regular and uniform** — no missing strands, no broken cells, no holes that aren't in the design.

## Visual style
- Clean, slightly out-of-focus neutral background (the product itself or a soft white gradient).
- Sharp focus on the feature area; gentle natural falloff elsewhere — no heavy artificial blur.

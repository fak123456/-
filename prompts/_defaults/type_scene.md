## Task
Generate one square Amazon EU lifestyle scene image that helps a buyer understand **where and how this product is used in real life**, while keeping the product clearly identifiable.

The visual approach is up to you — judge per scene what works best.

## Composition approach — pick what fits this specific scene
You may use any of the following styles, and you should choose whichever serves THIS scene best. Do not force the same style on every scene image in the batch.

**A. Product naturally in the scene (no inset).** The product appears at a believable size inside the environment — installed, mounted, being used, sitting on a surface. The product remains visually clear and recognizable on its own. This is the cleanest choice when the product is already large/central enough in the scene to be read at a glance.

**B. Scene + small product inset overlay.** The main canvas is the lifestyle scene; a small clean product hero shot sits in **one corner** as a callout. Use this only when the product would otherwise be hard to identify from the scene alone — e.g., it's far away, partially hidden, or visually similar to its surroundings. If you use an inset:
- Keep it **small and unobtrusive — roughly 15–25% of canvas width**. Never larger than 30%.
- Place it in **one of the four corners only** (bottom-left, bottom-right, top-left, or top-right). Pick whichever composes best with this specific scene. The bottom-left and bottom-right corners are usually the safest choices.
- Pick whichever **corner shape** composes best — a rounded rectangle, a small square, or a small circle. Do NOT use a horizontal banner strip or any edge-spanning band; corner-only.
- Inside the inset: a clean studio hero shot of the product on white/light background, with a thin neutral border or soft drop shadow so it visibly separates from the scene.

**C. Hybrid.** Product present in the scene, with a subtle small inset for clarity if needed.

### Important: variety across the batch
If multiple scene images in this batch end up using inset overlays, the **shape, position, and size** of those insets must differ between images so the gallery does not look templated. If a scene works without an inset, prefer that.

## Visual style
- Photorealistic real-world environment matching the scene brief.
- Natural lighting appropriate to the scene (sunlight outdoors, soft daylight indoors).
- If you use an inset: studio lighting inside the inset, color temperature matching the references.
- Color of the product (whether in the scene, in the inset, or both) must be identical to the references.

## Hard constraints
- Same product identity (color, shape, materials, parts, count) as references, wherever the product appears in the image.
- Faces of any recognizable person must NOT be shown clearly. Hands, body, or out-of-focus silhouettes are OK.
- No marketing text overlays, no price tags, no badges, no callouts, no arrows.
- No logos or brand graphics beyond what physically exists on the real product in the references.
- Trustworthy and real — no surreal, fantasy, or cartoon styling.

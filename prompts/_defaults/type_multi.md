## Task
Compose **one single square image** that combines:
1. a **multi-scene immersive background** showing **4 different real-world usage scenes** for this product, blended into one canvas, AND
2. a **prominent centered product hero callout** placed on top of that background.

Use the product title above and the reference images to decide what kind of product this is and which 4 usage scenes / environments / viewpoints make sense for THIS specific product. Do not invent settings unrelated to the product category. Stay strictly within plausible real-world uses of this product.

## Layout (mandatory)

### Background — exactly 4 different usage scenes, visually merged
- The background fills the **entire canvas, edge to edge**.
- It shows **exactly 4 visibly distinct usage scenes / environments / viewpoints** for this product. The 4 scenes are recognizable as 4 different settings at a glance.
- The 4 scenes are blended together using **soft organic transitions** — soft fades, depth-of-field falloff, light beams, diagonal curved boundaries, or smooth gradients. NOT a 2×2 hard grid, NOT separated rectangular cells with gutters, NOT obvious collage panels.
- Each of the 4 scenes shows either the product itself or the device/context the product is used in, **doing something relevant** for that scene. The product appearances must all match the references in color, materials, geometry, and unit count.
- The 4 scenes should cover **clearly different aspects** of the product's usage. Choose what makes sense for the product category, for example any 4 of:
  - a different physical location (room A vs room B, indoor vs outdoor, etc.),
  - a different time of day or lighting (daylight, evening, etc., only if relevant),
  - a different viewpoint or angle on the same kind of use,
  - a different stage of use (before / during / after, or installation / running / cleaning),
  - a different user context (one user vs multiple users, set vs single unit).
- Lighting and color grading per scene should match that scene naturally; overall the canvas should still feel cohesive (consistent saturation and contrast level).

### Center product hero callout
- A clean, prominent product hero placed at the **geometric center** of the canvas, overlaid on top of the background.
- Shape: a **circle, an oval / ellipse, or a soft rounded shape** with smooth edges. NOT a hard square or hard rectangle.
- Size: approximately **35–50% of the canvas width**.
- Inside the callout: a clean studio hero shot of the product on **pure white or very light neutral background**, with product identity exactly matching the references (same color, materials, geometry, parts, unit count). The product fills 70–85% of the callout.
- Frame: a thin neutral border or soft drop shadow around the callout so it visibly separates from the background scenes.

## Visual hierarchy
- The centered product hero is the FIRST thing the eye lands on.
- The 4 background scenes are the supporting visual context.
- The two layers must feel cohesive — matching color temperature and overall mood.

## Hard constraints
- Background MUST show **exactly 4 visibly different usage scenes** for this specific product. A single uniform scene is NOT acceptable. 2 or 3 scenes is NOT acceptable.
- The 4 scenes must be merged with **organic soft transitions**, NOT a 2×2 grid, NOT a 4-panel collage, NOT rectangular cells with gutters.
- One centered product hero callout, **circle / oval / soft rounded** shape, ~35–50% of canvas width.
- Product identity (color, materials, geometry, parts, unit count) must match the references in BOTH the centered callout AND every appearance of the product in any of the 4 background scenes.
- The 4 scenes must be **plausible real-world settings for this product**, derived from the product title and reference images. No invented or unrelated settings.
- No marketing text overlays, no price tags, no badges, no callout arrows, no logos beyond what is physically on the real product in the references.
- No faces of recognizable people; hands or body silhouettes are fine.
- No surreal / fantasy / cartoon styling.

## Negative
single uniform background scene, only 1 viewpoint shown, fewer than 4 sub-scenes, 2x2 grid, 4-panel collage, separated rectangular cells with gutters, hero placed off-center, hero too small (under 30%) or too big (over 55%), square / hard-corner hero box, scenes unrelated to the product category, invented or implausible settings, mismatched product variants between hero and background, text overlays, watermark, faces, cluttered scene, color drift, wrong product variant, cartoon style, surreal environment
